Researcher

Application Deadline: February 15, 2019

We're looking for a researcher to join our team and help us develop new insights into moral circle expansion. This position is ideal for a doctoral graduate or other early-career researcher with an interest in moral circle expansion. This position offers a large amount of flexibility with few requirements other than the production of high-quality research that fits within our mission. We are open to a variety of social science methods, qualitative (e.g. historical case studies, interviews, conceptual analysis) or quantitative (e.g. nationally representative surveys, behavioral experiments, analysis of existing industry and survey data). Since founding SI in 2017, we have primarily focused on farmed animals as the current frontier of humanity's moral circle. We expect future research in this area to be similar to our previous research, which is listed on this page. We have some ideas for future research listed in our research agenda that may also be of interest.

However, given our three years of research that has grabbed some of the low-hanging fruit in this area, we are also open to research projects on other moral circle expansion topics. The main other area we have in mind here is artificial sentience: robots, machines, and other entities made by humans that are able to experience happiness or suffering. For example, SI may run a nationally representative survey of US adults on their attitudes towards artificial sentience and related topics. In addition to artificial sentience, examples of possible research topics include the moral inclusion of wild animals and historical examples of moral circle expansion (analyzed without a specific current or future expansion in mind).

While we want all of our research to be thoroughly vetted and publically available, we are open to different publication formats. Our previous publications include reports (i.e. white papers, working papers), academic journal articles, blog posts, newspaper and magazine articles, and a book. The best publication format depends partly on the content and audience of the work, but we also want to consider how we can support early-career researchers working on the topic of moral circle expansion. In addition to research publication, we are open to other research-related activities, including but not limited to conference presentations, interviews and media commentary, meetings, and administrative work.

Employment type: Full-time (US "at-will" or UK "permanent"); we may consider a contract or half-time role for exceptional candidates.

Location: Remote, US-based (we may be able to make arrangements for international hires, but we are unable to sponsor US visas); current staff are in NYC and London.

Experience type: Several years of professional or academic social science research.

Reports to: Research Director

Work style: Relatively independent with frequent peer review and feedback and some collaboration

Salary: $45,000-50,000 (or international equivalent per costs of living) dependent on qualifications and need; negotiable; no benefits

Paid time off: 28 days per year (all-inclusive)

Work schedule: Flexible

Professional development: Employees will have opportunities to attend conferences and seminars relevant to their work

As a researcher, you will generally:

  • Collaborate with our team to decide what projects, especially those on our research agenda, to work on.
  • Conduct that research fairly independently, typically with regular check-ins and peer review prior to publication. Frequent questions and meetings will be expected at the beginning, but should slow down over time as you become more familiar with the research literature and methods.
  • Provide assistance as needed with other research projects and ideas, mainly by reviewing research for accuracy, clarity, quality of the reasoning, and overlooked evidence.

Required qualifications:

  • Several years of research and writing experience in academia, think tanks, or another relevant institution.
  • Expertise in specific research methods, such as designing, running, and analyzing surveys and behavioral experiments.
  • General expertise in research, such as being able to read and understand a wide range of qualitative and quantitative literature.
  • Strong critical reasoning ability.
  • Comfort with updating your views when presented with new information.
  • Ability to ask questions that move your projects forward and improve their quality.
  • Good written communication skills.
  • Ability to work mostly independently with limited structure or mentorship, with a willingness to report to and, at times, defer to others in the organization.

Preferred attributes (we encourage you to apply even if you don't meet these):

  • Graduate-level academic education in a social science field.
  • Comfort with making big-picture research decisions and navigating very open-ended research questions.
  • Comfort with speculation and making judgements based on limited evidence when stronger evidence is not available.
  • Familiarity with the animal advocacy movement and its strategies, and particularly with the debates and research on effective animal advocacy.
  • Familiarity with effective altruism.

Interview process, for your information:

  • Complete the application form below, including a short statement of research interests.
  • If we're interested in moving forward, we'll contact you to schedule an interview and give you questions to prepare for discussion.
  • After the interview, if we're interested in moving forward, we'll provide a short (max 2 hours) trial project.
  • After that, we may contact you with further questions before we make our final decision.
